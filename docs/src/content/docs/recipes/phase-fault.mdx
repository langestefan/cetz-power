A textbook diagram of a single-phase earth fault on an isolated
("floating") medium-voltage network. The three phase conductors
emerge from the source transformer through their winding inductances
on the left, the phase-to-ground capacitances are sketched as three
shunt capacitors hanging from the conductors, and the fault on phase
c is marked with a stylised lightning bolt returning to the ground
rail at the bottom. The four colours (red coils, blue cap leads,
black phase wires, green ground) match the source figure.

## How the colours map to the symbols

| Colour | Used for                                                                            |
| ------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Red    | The three winding inductors `La`/`Lb`/`Lc`, the per-phase fault-current arrows, the lightning bolt and `Ikt` label. |
| Black  | The phase wires `a` / `b` / `c`, the source-transformer enclosure, and the capacitor plates. |
| Blue   | The vertical leads from each phase down to its phase-to-ground capacitor.           |
| Green  | The ground rail at the bottom of the figure (the earth-conductor reference).        |

The lightning-bolt symbol is hand-drawn from a six-point polyline
with `mark: (end: ">", fill: red)` for the arrowhead — there's no
built-in `lightning-bolt` symbol, but a custom polyline is short
enough to inline in the snippet.

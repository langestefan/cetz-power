A real-world wind-turbine single-line: the external grid on the left
feeds a step-down transformer, reactive compensation sits at the tower
base (*mastvoet*), and the induction generator lives in the nacelle
(*gondel*). Every bus is annotated with a voltage and flagged with
the active/reactive power flowing into each of its connections — the
conventional way to read a load-flow result.

## Things worth noticing

- **Bespoke symbols via raw CeTZ.** cetz-power doesn't ship a rotating
  machine circle or a capacitor bank, so the snippet defines a tiny
  `machine(pos, letter)` helper (a stroked circle plus a character) and
  draws the capacitor plates with two `cetz.draw.line` calls. This is
  the intended extension path — the package's own symbols are thin
  wrappers over CeTZ, and you can mix them freely.
- **Custom bus label placement.** The `Mastvoet` label can't sit at
  `north` of its bus because the capacitor stem drops straight onto
  `b3.end`. It's emitted with `cetz.draw.content` anchored to
  `south-east` of a coordinate slightly above the bar, putting it in
  the free space to the upper-left of the stem.
- **Flow-label helper.** `flow(pos, mw, mvar, side)` keeps the
  MW/Mvar text blocks right-aligned on the left side of a bus and
  left-aligned on the right, so adjacent labels don't visually merge.
- **Capacitor stem goes to `b3.end`, not `b3.mid`.** `end` is the top
  of a vertical bus (the bus extends from `start` at the bottom to
  `end` at the top in its local frame, before rotation), which is
  where you want things that branch upward off the bar to attach.

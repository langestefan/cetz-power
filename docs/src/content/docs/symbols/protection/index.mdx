title: Protection & switching
description: Switchgear and overcurrent protection — switches, circuit breakers, fuses. The elements you draw between buses to control and protect feeders.
---

The protection family covers switchgear and overcurrent-protection
elements: anything you draw inline between two connection points to
control current flow or interrupt it on a fault. They share a few
conventions:

- All are **two-node** symbols. Pass an `in` and an `out` coordinate
  (or anchor); the body sits at the midpoint and the symbol orients
  itself along the in→out line, drawing its own lead stubs to the
  endpoints.
- The `in` and `out` anchors are exposed for further wiring.
- Each component has its own family in `set-style(cetz-power: …)`,
  so global, family, and per-call overrides cascade exactly the same
  way as for buses, transformers, and the rest of the package (see
  [Styling](/getting-started/styling/)).

## Currently shipped

| Symbol                                            | What it draws                                                                                |
| ------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| [Switch](/symbols/protection/switch/)             | Two-pin switch with a movable bar — open by default, `closed: true` for the closed pose.     |
| [Circuit breaker](/symbols/protection/breaker/)   | Square box on a wire — the conventional SLD notation distinguishing a CB from a disconnector.|
| [Fuse](/symbols/protection/fuse/)                 | Rectangle with a wire passing through it — IEC overcurrent-protection notation.              |

## Source layout

The symbol sources live under `src/symbols/protection/`. Future
additions (load-break switches, earthing switches, surge arresters,
recloser, …) belong here.
